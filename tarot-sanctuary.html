Tarot, lit by candlelight.

Move your pointer and a warm key light rakes across the card — gilt linework catches, matte ink stays quiet. The trick: a transparent WebGL2 canvas over the card, shading normal / roughness / height maps per pixel. Every card and every map is drawn in-page on canvas — this file loads zero image assets.

How the effect works

Four pieces, all vanilla JS — no libraries, no build step, no textures fetched over the network.

gen / canvas-pbr

Procedural card + maps

Each card's linework is drawn once on a 2D canvas, then tinted gold for the diffuse. The height map is that same ink blurred; normals come from its gradients; roughness drops wherever there's gilt.

gl / key-light

Pointer-driven candle

A fragment shader lights the card with a warm key that follows the cursor: wrapped diffuse, roughness-shaped specular, height-boosted sheen, and a faint rim when the light rakes from the side.

css / tilt

3D tilt + shadow

The mount sways idly and leans a few degrees toward the pointer (rotateX/rotateY). The drop shadow slides away from the light, lagging with the same easing as the tilt.

seq / draw

Draw choreography

Drawing spawns a fixed-position clone that flips to its back and sails off the top-left, while the real card — parked below the hero, back showing — rises from bottom center and flips over as it settles.

▎i

Live-tune the lighting from the console: CardLighting.set('uSpecStr', 0.6) — or CardLighting.back.set(...) for the card back. Uniforms: uParallax, uLightZ, uNormalStr, uSpecStr, uDiffuseAmt, uAmbientAmt, uLightColor.
