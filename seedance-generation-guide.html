01

What you're actually working with.

Briefing

Seedance 2.0 is a multimodal video model from ByteDance's Seed Lab, launched February 12, 2026. You give it text, images, video clips, and audio references — in any combination — and it returns a short film with synchronized sound. The headline move: instead of writing increasingly desperate prompts, you show the model what you want by uploading references.

Capacity in a single generation

You can stack references across modalities — the model figures out the role of each on its own.

9 × IMAGE

Character faces, environments, style anchors, color palettes.

3 × VIDEO

Camera moves, choreography, editing rhythm. Up to ~15s total.

3 × AUDIO

Background music, sound effects, dialogue samples for sync.

+ TEXT

Scene description, camera direction, pacing, performance notes.

03

The five-step shoot.

Workflow

Same loop on every platform — only the buttons change. Treat the first generation as a polaroid: it tells you which dial to turn next, not whether you're done.

i.

Pick a mode that matches your strongest asset.

Have a vivid mental image and no files? Text-to-Video. Have a single still you want to animate? Image-to-Video. Have a character photo plus a dance clip plus a beat? Multimodal/References. The model can accept up to 9 images, 3 videos, and 3 audio files in one go — but start with whichever asset best anchors your idea.

Tip: start simple, layer references on retry
ii.

Upload references, name them if the platform supports it.

On platforms that surface the @-reference system, label each asset (@Image1, @Video1, @Audio1) so your prompt can point to specific files. Keep the labels consistent across edits — if the model has to guess which file is which, you'll get drift.

iii.

Write a structured prompt — not a paragraph.

Walls of text confuse the model. Use the formula in the next section. Keep it under 200 words, lead with the subject and action, end with style and constraints. Reference your uploaded files by name where they apply.

iv.

Set duration and resolution low for the first pass.

Generate 5 seconds at 720p while you're still iterating — shorter clips render faster and cost fewer free credits per attempt. Once the motion, composition, and reference fidelity are right, bump to 1080p and your final length (up to 15s).

5s @ 720p → iterate · 10s @ 1080p → final
v.

Generate three variants. Change one thing at a time.

The single biggest habit for consistent results: never change camera, subject, and lighting on the same retry. You won't know what helped. Hold everything constant, move one dial, compare. Then move the next.

04

The prompt recipe.

Craft

Seedance 2.0 rewards specificity. The structure below covers the six dimensions the model can actually act on — subject, setting, motion, camera, style, and constraint. Skip any of them and the model fills the gap, usually not the way you'd hope.

Subject+Setting → Action → Camera move → Style & lighting → Pacing → (optional) Negatives
★ Text-to-video

A young woman in a white linen dress steps onto a rooftop at golden hour. She walks slowly toward the railing, hair lifting in a soft breeze. Camera tracks behind her at waist height, then cranes up to reveal the city skyline. Cinematic, warm backlight, shallow depth of field, 35mm film grain. Slow, contemplative pacing.

★ Image-to-video

Animate @Image1 as the first frame. The subject turns slowly to face the camera, expression softening into a small smile. Slight handheld camera motion. Natural window light from the left. Subtle dust particles in the air. 5 seconds, intimate, documentary tone.

★ Multimodal reference

Character from @Image1 dances in the style of @Video1. Outfit references @Image2. Sync the rhythm to @Audio1. Setting: neon-lit alleyway at night, steam rising from grates. Camera circles the subject. Music-video style.

★ Product shot

A sleek wireless headphone floats and rotates against a pure black background. Soft studio lighting from above-right creates elegant reflections on the matte surface. Smooth 360-degree rotation over 6 seconds. Premium commercial style, 4K cinematic quality. No text overlays, no watermark.

05

The @-reference trick.

Multimodal

The feature that actually sets Seedance 2.0 apart: every uploaded file gets a handle you can call out in plain text. Think of it as casting — you're not describing a dance, you're pointing at one.

What each reference type controls

@Image1

Character identity, outfit, environment, color palette, or first/last frame anchor.

@Video1

Camera movement, choreography, editing rhythm, visual effects style. The model extracts motion, not pixels.

@Audio1

Background music, sound effects, or a beat to sync visual rhythm to. Native audio means lip-sync also works.

"reference @X's …"

Be explicit about what to take from each file. "Reference @Video1's camera move" tells the model to ignore the subjects in that clip.

06

Habits of the people getting good output.

Practice
01 / KEEP IT SHORT
Cap prompts under 200 words.

The model handles concise, structured prompts better than long descriptive paragraphs. Cut adjectives that aren't doing work.

02 / IMAGE FIRST
If you have a still, start there.

Image-to-Video gives the model more to anchor to than text alone. Consistency improves noticeably when there's a reference frame.

03 / NEGATIVES
State what you don't want.

"No text overlays, no watermark, no extra fingers" — quick exclusions catch failure modes the model would otherwise reach for.

04 / ONE DIAL
Change one variable per retry.

Swap the camera move, or the lighting, or the subject — never all three. Otherwise you can't tell what fixed it.

05 / CINEMA VOCAB
Use real camera language.

"Dolly in," "low-angle handheld," "fisheye," "rack focus." The model was trained on film descriptions; speak the dialect.

06 / TIMING WORDS
Pacing matters more than you think.

"Slowly zooms in," "snap cut to," "long unbroken take." Tempo instructions shape both visual rhythm and the audio sync.

07 / DRAFT MODE
Iterate at 720p, finish at 1080p.

You'll save credits and time. Reserve the higher resolution for the take you're actually keeping.

08 / NAME CONSISTENTLY
Don't rename references mid-edit.

If you call the same asset @Image1 in one prompt and @Reference in the next, you're making the model guess.

09 / EXTEND, DON'T REGENERATE
Use the extend feature on clips you like.

Seedance can continue a generated clip with consistent characters and motion — cheaper than redoing the whole thing for length.

07

When it goes wrong.

Debug
The character's face changes mid-clip.
Add a reference image of the face and call it out explicitly: "Maintain the appearance of @Image1 throughout." Drop the duration to 5–8 seconds — longer clips drift more.
Motion looks like a slideshow.
Your prompt is probably static. Add a verb of motion ("walks," "spins," "tracks across") and a camera instruction. The model defaults to minimal movement when neither is specified.
The generate button is greyed out.
A reference doesn't meet input specs — usually image resolution, aspect ratio, or file size. Hover the button on most platforms; it'll tell you which file is the problem.
The audio doesn't match the action.
Either upload a reference audio clip and tell the prompt to sync to it, or be more specific about sound in the prompt itself: "footsteps on gravel," "low ambient hum." Native audio responds to description, not just vibes.
The model refused my prompt.
Third-party content policy filters block requests involving public figures, brand likenesses, explicit content, and certain violence. Reword toward generic descriptions ("a tall man in a suit") rather than names.
All my free credits are gone.
Most platforms reset daily. Stack accounts across Dreamina, Jimeng, ImagineArt, and OpenArt to extend your free runway. Or batch all your generations into one session per day.
